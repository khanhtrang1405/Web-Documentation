Reflections on proposal

From the user feedback of the proposal, here are some of the issues found:

  • Except from the call-to-action buttons (‘Donate’ and ‘Volunteer’), other buttons don’t stand out.
  • Color contrast (e.g: light yellow on white, light green on white, etc.) doesn’t make the content stand out and affect the visibility of users.
  • Although white space was mentioned a lot in the sketches to make the pages more spacious, the mockups are more clustered due to the use of green sections.
  • The tab metaphor in ‘Educate’, ‘News’ and ‘Campaigns’ pages isn’t shown clearly.
  • Accessibility in form is also an issue as fields don’t have a consistent and visible label at all times.

Next step

Following the user feedback, changes will be made accordingly to improve the website prototype

Moreover, due to the page limit, I’ve decided to not include the ‘Educate’ and ‘News’ pages in the ‘Solar Energy’ section. I want to focus more on showcasing what Solar Citizens does and what they have achieved, addressing the motivations of the user profiles deducted from the research. As a result, it helps inform users about the transparency of the NGO which is what users require and need.

Similarly, the contact page is kept but as a blank page while the general information (address, contact and social media links) is brought to the footer so they can access from any pages. This is because the ‘Donate’ and ‘Volunteer’ pages already have a form for users to submit their contact details and other information which the brief requires. In addition, the general information is sufficient enough for users to find the contact info of the organization.

Iterations

Landing Page

Iteration 1

Landing page is very important as it functions as the key portal of the website as well as the foundation for the design of other pages.

As I want to give the page more white space, I first tried to give the background with just one color and no sections. I’ve tried with 3 different colors: dark green, light green and light yellow and adjust the text colors to contrast with the background accordingly. I also added a shadow to the photo slideshow to give the page more dimension.

Iteration 2

However, when showing this this one-color idea to my friends and asked whether the website attracts their attention, they all agree that the page feels flat with no highlights and sectioning. As a result, I revisited my sketches tried to play with the vertical sectioning instead of horizontal way in the previous version. In this way, I can still make the content and slideshow stand out individually without feeling so clustered. For the colors of these sections, I chose the light yellow and dark green to make it even more contrasted. I experimented with various shapes, from rectangle, rounded rectangle to oval. I also played with the position of navigation bar, between left and center.

Iteration 3

when showing these four versions to 5 of my friends and asked which one they like the most, they unanimously voted for the enlarged half oval as it gives the page a more balanced layout between the boxy slideshow and a more organic background shape. However, some said that the slideshow doesn't stand put because of the green background therefore, a rounded yellow border is added, giving it even more depth. With the navigation bar, I decided to keep it centered. By doing so, the layout is kept balanced and the consistency in pages with the centralised layout is maintained. Moreover, this separates the bar into 3 parts: logo, information and take action, helping the audience to locate easier.

About Page

Iteration 1

The biggest problem found in this page is the visibility of the description text on top of the background image since it has so many colors. Although I’ve experimented with changing the opacity of the photo or a color box behind the text, it still looks very featureless with no distinctive features.

Iteration 2

I then explore the layout from the landing page by dividing into the content and image sections. During user testing, some participants feel like the layout is unbalanced and the content and information isn’t sufficient. One participant states that if this website wants to show the most transparency, more information needs to be added to gain people's trust.
